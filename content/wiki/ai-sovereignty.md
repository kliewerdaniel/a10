# AI Sovereignty

AI sovereignty refers to the ability of individuals, organizations, and nations to control and govern their use of artificial intelligence technologies. It encompasses the principles and practices that ensure data privacy, security, ethical considerations, and self-determination in the deployment and management of AI systems.

## Core Principles

1. **Data Privacy** — Organizations ensure their data remains within their control
2. **Ethical Alignment** — AI systems are designed to align with specific ethical guidelines and values
3. **Self-Determination** — Entities choose AI technologies that fit their needs without external constraints
4. **Local Control** — Utilizing decentralized solutions enables autonomous AI operations

## The Sovereignty Stack

- **Models**: Open-source LLMs (Llama, Qwen) running locally
- **Vector Stores**: ChromaDB, Qdrant, Milvus for independent data management
- **Infrastructure**: Self-hosted or on-premises deployment
- **Governance**: Custom policies and ethical frameworks

## Why It Matters

- Avoids vendor lock-in with centralized AI providers
- Protects sensitive data from leaving your infrastructure
- Enables alignment with local regulations and cultural values
- Fosters innovation through self-determination
